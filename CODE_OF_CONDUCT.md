# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

In the interest of fostering an open and welcoming environment, we as contributors and maintainers pledge to make participation in our project and our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

## Our Standards

### Examples of behavior that contributes to creating a positive environment include:

- Using welcoming and inclusive language
- Being respectful of differing viewpoints and experiences
- Gracefully accepting constructive criticism
- Focusing on what is best for the community and the project
- Showing empathy towards other community members
- Being patient with team members who are learning
- Providing helpful and constructive feedback
- Celebrating the successes of others

### Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- The use of sexualized language or imagery and unwelcome sexual attention or advances
- Trolling, insulting/derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks
- Public or private harassment
- Publishing others' private information, such as a physical or electronic address, without explicit permission
- Condescending or patronizing behavior
- Dismissing or attacking someone for lacking knowledge or experience
- Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting

## Our Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying the standards of acceptable behavior and are expected to take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any instances of unacceptable behavior.

Project maintainers have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned to this Code of Conduct, or to ban temporarily or permanently any contributor for other behaviors that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces, including:

- GitHub repository (issues, pull requests, discussions)
- Project documentation
- Communication channels (email, chat, meetings)
- Project events and presentations

This Code of Conduct also applies when an individual is representing the project or its community in public spaces.

## Academic Integrity

As this is an academic project developed for a Software Engineering course:

### Expected Academic Conduct

- **Original Work**: All code and documentation must be original work or properly attributed
- **Collaboration**: Collaboration among team members is encouraged and expected
- **Attribution**: Properly cite external resources, libraries, and code snippets
- **Learning**: Focus on learning and understanding, not just completing tasks
- **Honesty**: Be honest about your contributions and limitations

### Prohibited Academic Conduct

- Plagiarism or copying code without attribution
- Using someone else's work and claiming it as your own
- Sharing solutions with other teams or classes (if applicable)
- Misrepresenting your contributions to the project

## Team Collaboration Guidelines

### Communication

- **Be Responsive**: Respond to messages and pull requests in a timely manner
- **Be Clear**: Communicate clearly and provide context
- **Be Professional**: Maintain professionalism in all communications
- **Ask Questions**: Don't hesitate to ask for clarification or help

### Meetings and Standups

- **Be Punctual**: Arrive on time for scheduled meetings
- **Be Prepared**: Come prepared with updates and questions
- **Be Concise**: Respect everyone's time
- **Be Engaged**: Actively participate in discussions

### Code Reviews

- **Be Respectful**: Provide feedback respectfully and constructively
- **Be Specific**: Clearly explain what needs to be changed and why
- **Be Helpful**: Suggest solutions, not just problems
- **Be Open**: Accept feedback graciously

### Conflict Resolution

- **Address Issues Early**: Don't let small issues grow into big problems
- **Be Direct**: Communicate directly with the person involved
- **Be Solution-Oriented**: Focus on finding solutions, not assigning blame
- **Seek Mediation**: Involve a mediator (instructor/TA) if needed

## Enforcement

### Reporting

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported by:

1. Contacting project maintainers
2. Reporting to the course instructor
3. Using GitHub's reporting features

All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly.

### Consequences

Project maintainers who do not follow or enforce the Code of Conduct in good faith may face temporary or permanent repercussions as determined by other members of the project's leadership or the course instructor.

### Enforcement Guidelines

1. **First Offense - Warning**
   - Private written warning
   - Clarification of the violation
   - Explanation of acceptable behavior

2. **Second Offense - Temporary Ban**
   - Temporary ban from project contributions
   - Required meeting with instructor/team lead
   - Written apology may be required

3. **Third Offense - Permanent Ban**
   - Permanent removal from the project
   - Referral to academic authorities if applicable

## Scope During Development Sprints

During each sprint:

- Respect sprint commitments and deadlines
- Communicate blockers or issues promptly
- Help team members who are struggling
- Celebrate sprint successes together
- Learn from sprint retrospectives

## Recognition and Appreciation

We encourage team members to:

- Recognize good work publicly
- Thank others for their contributions
- Celebrate milestones and achievements
- Support each other's learning and growth
